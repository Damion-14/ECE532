$$ \| x - T w \|_2^2 = (x - T w)^T (x - T w) $$ $$ (x - T w)^T (x - T w) = (x^T - (Tw)^T) (x - T w) $$ $$ (x^T - (Tw)^T) (x - T w) = (x^T - w^TT^T) (x - T w) $$ $$ (x^T - w^TT^T) (x - T w) = x^Tx - x^TTw - w^TT^Tx + w^TT^TTw \quad \text{fact: } - x^TTw - w^TT^Tx = -2w^TT^Tx $$ $$ x^Tx -2w^TT^Tx + w^TT^TTw = x^Tx -2w^TT^Tx + w^Tw $$ $$ \frac{\partial}{\partial w} \left( x^T x - 2 w^T T^T x + w^T w \right) = 0 $$ $$ - 2T^T x + 2w = 0 $$ $$ w = T^Tx $$

# from wikipedia
def gram_schmidt(V):
    """ Applies Gram-Schmidt orthogonalization to matrix V. """
    n, k = V.shape
    U = np.zeros((n, k))
    for i in range(n):
        U[:, i] = V[:, i]
        for j in range(i):
            U[:, i] = U[:, i] -  np.dot(U[:, j], V[:, i]) * U[:, j]  # Subtract projections
        U[:, i] = U[:, i] / np.linalg.norm(U[:, i])  # Normalize
    return U

Question 4

Since $ Q $ is symmetric ($ Q = Q^T $): $$ QPQ = Q^T P Q. $$

$ \forall x \in \mathbb{R}^n \quad x \neq 0 $: $$ x^T (QPQ) x = x^T Q^T P Q x. $$ Let $ y = Qx $: $$ x^T Q^T P Q x = y^T P y. $$ Since $ P \succ 0 \Rightarrow y^T P y > 0 $ for all $ y \neq 0 $: $$ x^T (QPQ) x = y^T P y > 0. $$

Since $ x^T (QPQ) x > 0 $ for all nonzero $ x $, $ QPQ $ is positive definite.
