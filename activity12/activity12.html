Question 2-b¶

We can create new variables for X and y:

$$ \hat{X} = \begin{bmatrix} X \\ \sqrt{\lambda}I \end{bmatrix} \hat{y} = \begin{bmatrix} y \\ 0 \end{bmatrix} $$

$$ \min_w{||\hat{y} - \hat{X}w||_2^2} $$

Question 2-c¶

For $ \min_w{||\hat{y} - \hat{X}w||_2^2} $ to have a unique solution $\hat{X}$ must have $rank = p$, $\lambda$ must also be $\gt 0$

Question 3-a¶

$$ X^TX = V \Sigma U^T U \Sigma V^T = V \Sigma^2 V^T $$ $$ (X^TX + \lambda I)^{-1}X^T = V(\Sigma^2 + \lambda I)^{-1} \Sigma U^T $$ $$ (\Sigma^2 + \lambda I)^{-1} = \frac{1}{\sigma^2_i + \lambda} $$ $$ (X^T X + \lambda I)^{-1} X^T = \sum_{i=1}^p \frac{1}{\sigma_i^2 + \lambda} v_i u_i^T $$

Question 3-b¶

$$ X^{\dagger} = \lim_{\lambda \to 0^+} (X^T X + \lambda I)^{-1} X^T $$ As $ \lambda \rightarrow 0 $ $$ (X^T X + \lambda I)^{-1} = (X^T X)^{-1} $$ Then
$$ X^{\dagger} = (X^T X)^{-1} X^T $$

Question 3-c¶

When X is sqaure $$ (X^T X)^{-1} = X^{-1}X^{-T} $$ $$ X^{-T}X^T = I $$ $$ X^{\dagger} = X^{-1} $$

Question 3-d¶

When r < p and only the first r singular values are nonzero so any terms calculated in the sum past r are 0 and basically ignored.
So we can simplify the expression from 3-a to only take the sum from 1 to r

Question 3-e¶

$$ X^{\dagger} = (X^T X)^{-1} X^T $$ As $ \lambda \rightarrow 0 $ $$ X^{\dagger} = \sum_{i=1}^r \frac{1}{\sigma_i} v_i u_i^T $$ Therefore $$ X^{\dagger} = V \Sigma_r^{-1} U^T $$
